# 06 — Competitors & positioning

Peer-to-peer space rental is proven in individual categories abroad, but **no one owns the
multi-asset, subletting-first, Nordic-native position.** That gap is the opportunity.

## Landscape

### Parking
- **Operators / payments (not P2P marketplaces):** EasyPark, Parkster, Aimo Park, Q-Park, Parkando (SE).
  They run or digitize *commercial* parking — they are not marketplaces for private idle spaces, and
  are potential **partners or acquirers**, not head-to-head rivals.
- **P2P parking marketplaces:** JustPark, YourParkingSpace (UK); SpotHero, Neighbor (US); Spacer (AU).
  These validate the model; none is Nordic-native or subletting-focused.

### Storage
- **Neighbor.com (US)** — "Airbnb for storage," >$100M raised. The strongest proof point for this
  whole thesis and a direct template for the storage category.
- **Stashbee (UK)** — P2P storage + parking.
- **Traditional self-storage** (Shurgard, 24Storage, etc.) — expensive, impersonal; the incumbent you undercut.

### Boat berths
- **Dockwa, Snag-A-Slip (US)**, and various European berth-booking tools — fragmented, often
  marina-operator software rather than P2P sublet of an allocated private berth.

### Multi-asset / general space
- **Spacer (AU/US)** — parking + storage, closest to the multi-asset idea, but not in the Nordics.
- **Stowga, various "space" B2B platforms** — warehouse/commercial, different segment.

### Nordic reality check
- The Nordic market is dominated by **parking operators and payment apps**, not P2P *private-space*
  marketplaces. Peer-to-peer subletting of leased parking/storage/berths is **underserved** — which is
  exactly the whitespace, but also a signal to validate demand hard in Phase 0 ([05](05-mvp-roadmap.md)).

> Verify the current competitive set at launch — this space moves. Names above are directional.

## How Platsdela stands out

1. **Multi-asset under one trust layer.** Competitors are single-category (parking *or* storage *or*
   berths). Platsdela is one identity, payments, insurance, and access stack across all of them —
   more supply per user, cross-category demand, and a defensible platform rather than a feature.
2. **Subletting-first, compliance as a feature.** Everyone else assumes owners. Platsdela is built for
   people who *lease*, with right-to-sublet verification and consent badges ([02](02-legal-and-compliance.md))
   that make an awkward legal reality into a trust advantage.
3. **Nordic-native.** BankID identity, Swish + card, Swedish/Nordic tax handling (incl. **VAT on
   parking** and **DAC7**), local language and support. Foreign P2P apps can't easily replicate this.
4. **B2B supply flywheel.** BRFs, property managers, and municipalities have large pools of idle,
   pre-allocated spaces where consent is automatic. Consumer-only apps ignore this; it's your cheapest,
   cleanest supply.
5. **Access without key exchange.** QR/keypad/LPR removes the biggest UX friction in physical-space
   rental and builds an audit trail for disputes.

## Positioning statement

> **For** people who lease parking, storage, garages, or boat berths they don't fully use,
> **Platsdela** is the marketplace that turns that idle, already-paid-for space into income —
> **unlike** single-category parking apps or commercial operators, it handles multiple space types
> with Nordic-native identity, payments, and built-in subletting compliance.

## Moats (what compounds over time)

- **Liquidity** in each geography (the classic marketplace moat).
- **Trust & compliance infrastructure** (BankID, consent verification, DAC7, insurance) — expensive to
  replicate and a barrier for foreign entrants.
- **B2B supply relationships** with BRFs/property managers — sticky and hard to dislodge.
- **Cross-category data** on pricing and demand across space types.
